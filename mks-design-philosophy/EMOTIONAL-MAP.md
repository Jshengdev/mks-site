# MKS Emotional Map

## The Journey Through the Site

This maps the intended emotional arc of a user's experience from arrival to departure.

```
ARRIVAL          IMMERSION          RECOGNITION          DESIRE          ACTION
  |                 |                    |                  |               |
  v                 v                    v                  v               v
[dark]         [atmosphere]         [the music]        [the object]    [the act]
[waiting]      [vastness opens]     [this is real]     [I want this]   [I have it]
[anticipation] [I'm inside it]     [someone made      [it's beautiful] [I'm going]
               [it's breathing]      this feeling]     [I can hold it]
```

---

## Emotional States by Section

### 1. The Threshold (Landing)

**State: Sacred anticipation**

The moment before you hear a piece of music you've been told will change you. The silence in a concert hall after the lights go down and before the first note. You don't know what's coming but your body is already preparing to receive it.

This is not excitement. It is the stillness that precedes something important.

**Design must create:**
- Slowness without boredom
- Darkness without emptiness
- The sense that something is about to happen

---

### 2. The Immersion (First Scroll)

**State: Being held by something larger than yourself**

Standing at the edge of the ocean at dusk. You are aware of your own smallness but it doesn't diminish you - it relieves you. You don't have to be big here. You can just receive.

The nature elements function here. Not as pictures of nature - as the feeling of being inside a vast, unhurried, living system.

**Design must create:**
- Scale that envelops
- Movement that breathes (not performs)
- The user feeling like a participant, not a viewer

---

### 3. The Recognition (Music Section)

**State: Oh - I know this feeling**

The moment a melody catches something in your chest and you realize it was already there, waiting. The music doesn't create the emotion. It finds it.

This is where the audience's reported experience lives - the forgotten memories, the childhood moments, the happy tears. The site doesn't need to manufacture this. The music does it. The site just needs to get out of the way and let the music reach the person.

**Design must create:**
- Clarity (the music must be easy to find and play)
- Intimacy (the transition from vastness to something personal)
- Permission (it's okay to feel this)

---

### 4. The Desire (Store)

**State: I want to hold this**

The intangible has become tangible. The feeling has a form - a CD case, a score sheet, a physical artifact. The condensation on the Coke bottle. You see it and your hands know what it would feel like to hold it.

This is not consumer impulse. This is the desire to keep something that moved you. To take a piece of an experience home.

**Design must create:**
- Tactile imagination (you can almost feel the object)
- Beauty that triggers care (you want to handle it gently)
- Simplicity of acquisition (wanting to buying has no friction)

---

### 5. The Invitation (Tour)

**State: This exists in the real world and I can be there**

The site has been a contained, curated world. The tour section breaks the fourth wall. This person is real. These places are real. This date is real. You could sit in that room and hear this music move through air and hit your actual body.

**Design must create:**
- Grounded reality after the dreamlike quality of the rest
- Urgency without pressure
- The feeling of an open door

---

## The Departure State

When someone leaves the site, they should carry:

- A sense of having been somewhere, not having browsed something
- The lingering quality of a film they just watched alone
- The quiet certainty that they'll come back
- If they bought something: the feeling of having chosen well
- If they didn't: the feeling of something unfinished (in a way that pulls them back)

## Words That Belong to This Brand

These are not taglines. They are the vocabulary of the emotional space:

| Word | Why |
|------|-----|
| Longing | The core emotional engine. Not sadness - the ache of reaching toward something beautiful. |
| Vast | The scale of feeling. Bigger than containable. The sky, the sea, the space inside a held note. |
| Return | Memories returning. The listener returning. Coming back to something you didn't know you'd left. |
| Surface | What you show vs. what lies beneath. The ocean metaphor. The cursor parting the water. |
| Held | Being held by music. Holding a CD. The safety of being inside something larger. |
| Emerge | How the name appears. How emotion surfaces. How a melody rises from silence. |
| Craft | The handwritten scores. The real sunsets. The refusal of the manufactured. |

## Words That Do NOT Belong

| Word | Why Not |
|------|---------|
| Epic | Too loud. Too Marvel. The feeling here is vast, not epic. |
| Vibes | Too casual. This is not a mood board. It's a world. |
| Content | The music is not content. The products are not content. They are works. |
| Drop | Music doesn't drop. It arrives. |
| Fire | No. |
| Aesthetic | The word has been emptied. Say what you mean instead. |
| Minimal | The design may be restrained but calling it minimal reduces it to a trend. |
