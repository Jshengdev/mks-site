# MKS Site Architecture

## Structural Overview

The site retains the current informational layout but transforms the experience layer. Contact is replaced with Store. Tour is added.

### Sections

| Section | Purpose | Emotional Function |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| **Landing / Hero** | First contact. The cinematic entrance. | Create the state of longing. Arrest attention. Establish that this is not a template. |
| **Music / Listen** | The catalog. Streaming links, featured releases. | The resolution. You've been moved - here's what moved you. Accessible, not buried. |
| **About / Story** | The human behind the sound. Bio, journey, context. | Connection. The audience already feels something - now they learn who caused it. |
| **Store** | Physical products. CDs, merch, collectibles. | Desire made tangible. The Coca-Cola moment. Products presented as objects of beauty, not inventory. |
| **Tour** | Live dates, venues, ticketing. | The invitation. You've heard it, you've felt it, you've held it - now come experience it in person. |

---

## The Landing Experience

### Sequence (Cinematic Pacing)

1. **Black.** The screen is dark. Not loading - waiting. A held moment.
2. **Atmosphere emerges.** Slowly, depth appears. Not an image loading in. A world forming - like eyes adjusting to darkness and realizing you're looking at the sky, or the sea, or a horizon you can't quite place.
3. **The name arrives.** MIKE THE KING SHENG. Not all at once. Letter by letter, or word by word, or as a slow luminous reveal. It doesn't announce. It becomes present.
4. **Sound option.** If music auto-plays or is offered: it begins at this moment. The visual and audio arrive together. One experience.
5. **The invitation to scroll.** Subtle. A shift in the atmosphere. A gentle motion downward. The user understands: there's more beneath this surface.

### Navigation

- Navigation should not feel like a toolbar. It should feel like a choice.
- Minimal labels. The sections are discoverable through the experience, not through a menu bar that breaks the atmosphere.
- Consider: navigation that appears on engagement (scroll, cursor movement) rather than on load. The first moment is sacred. Don't clutter it.

## Store Philosophy

### Products as Art Objects

The store does not look like an e-commerce grid. Each product is presented the way a gallery presents a piece:

- **Single product focus.** One item visible at a time, or items with significant breathing room.
- **The CD presentation.** CDs are photographed/presented as physical objects with weight, texture, dimension. Not flat thumbnails. You should almost feel the case in your hands.
- **Context over specs.** Instead of "Track listing: 1. Song A, 2. Song B" - present the album with the emotional language it deserves. What world does this album take you to?
- **The purchase moment.** Buying should feel like acquiring a piece of something meaningful, not checking out of a cart.

### Score Sheets as Product

The handwritten scores are unique to MKS. They are both artistic artifacts and products. Their presentation should emphasize:
- The physicality of paper and ink
- The intimacy of handwriting
- The rarity - these are not mass-produced

## Tour Section

### Live Experience Framing

Tour dates are not a list. They are invitations.

- Each date presented with a sense of place. The venue, the city - treated with the same atmospheric quality as the rest of the site.
- The feeling: "this is happening, in this place, and you could be there."
- Clear, functional ticketing access. The beauty of the site never gets in the way of someone being able to actually go.

## Interaction Design Principles

| Principle | Implementation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Discover, don't direct** | Let the user feel like they're finding things, not being funneled. |
| **Reward attention** | Hidden details, subtle animations, things that only appear if you linger. |
| **Respect the pace** | Nothing instant. Nothing jarring. Every transition has the cadence of a long exhale. |
| **Sound as design element** | If audio is incorporated, it is not supplementary. It is architectural. The site without sound should work. The site with sound should transform. |
| **Mobile as intimate** | On phone, the experience becomes even more personal. Closer. The screen is held in your hands like a photograph. Design for that intimacy. |
