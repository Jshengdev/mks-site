# MKS Style Decisions — Locked In

Extracted from the style finder session. These are final. Build from these.

---

## 1. Temperature: COLD WORLD, WARM MOMENTS

The site is dark, cool, teal-ocean-steel as its baseline. Warmth appears only through interaction or at specific emotional peaks (the store, hover states, the music activating). The gold is rare and earned. The dramatic version.

> "I like the one that's more dramatically visual."
> "That vibe of the music definitely feels it."

**Rule:** Default state is cold. Warmth is the reward for engagement.

---

## 2. Nature Element: ALL OF THEM, ROTATING

Every nature element belongs — ocean, sky, grass, clouds, sunsets — but they rotate depending on the section or context. Like the four seasons. Each section of the site gets its own nature language.

> "All of them but you can rotate depending on the song or something."
> "That's sick because then you can get all the variety in it."

**Rule:** Nature is not one mood. It's a rotating vocabulary. Match the element to the emotional state of the section.

**Suggested mapping:**
- Landing: Sky / clouds (liberation, vastness)
- Music: Ocean (depth, what lies beneath)
- About: Grass field / plains (openness, the journey)
- Store: Golden hour / sunset (warmth, desire, the threshold)
- Tour: Night sky / city through windows (the gathering)
- Footer: Dusk haze (the fade, the aftermath)

---

## 3. Score Sheets: HIDDEN LAYER (Option B)

The scores are not front-and-center decoration. They exist beneath the surface — revealed through cursor interaction, the ocean-parting concept. You have to engage to find them. They're the secret beneath the water.

> "Definitely option B. I'm a fan of more interactive interaction UX and metaphorical things."
> "You want to be low-key minimalistic but still capture that feeling."

**Rule:** Scores are never wallpaper. They are discovered. The user's attention is what reveals them.

---

## 4. Piano: FLOWERS, GOLDEN LIGHT

The tender version. Organic meets mechanical. Vulnerability over rigor. The piano with flowers, not the clinical straight-on mechanism.

> "Piano flower is definitely the tender vulnerable organic mechanical romantic feel."

**Rule:** When the instrument appears, it appears with life inside it. Not documentation — poetry.

---

## 5. Performance Shots: INSIDE THE AUDIENCE

The POV is from within the crowd, looking toward the performer. The user is placed among the people, not observing from outside.

> "Put yourself in my shoes type of visual but inside the audience one."

**Rule:** The audience perspective, always. The viewer is a participant, not a spectator.

---

## 6. Landing Page: THE SCORE IN THE SKY

Not just the sky. Not just the score. The score flying through the sky. The music unbound from the page, liberated into the vastness. The theme is liberation.

> "What if my score is flying through the sky or something?"
> "The music is freeing and liberating."
> "Liberation. My leash."

**Rule:** The landing page merges two worlds — the handwritten craft (the score) and the infinite natural world (the sky). The score is not pinned to a desk. It's airborne. The music has escaped the page.

**The image in Mike's mind:** Cloud mass with rays behind it. A figure carrying a music scroll with a white horse behind him. The scroll is the craft — the handwritten scores, the body of work, carried forward. This is the personal mythology. The cinematic self-image. Build toward this.

---

## 7. Interaction Level: MEDIUM-TO-HEAVY

Not minimal (too passive). Not full art-installation (alienates part of the audience). Medium with heavy tendencies — scroll-triggered reveals, cursor interactions, some experimental moments. Lean toward "woah this is cool" for the younger audience. The boomers are not the website's primary audience.

> "Capturing younger audience who are like 'woah this is cool' would pay dividends."
> "Your music could hit that audience."

**Rule:** The site should make a 22-year-old stop scrolling and send the link to a friend. Older audiences can still navigate it, but the experience layer targets the person who's never heard of MKS and needs to be arrested.

**Also noted:** Spanish version at `/es`. Needs translator.

---

## 8. Store: CLEAN CATALOG, ELEVATED PRODUCTS

Clean grid for structure, but each product lives in its own world. Individual CDs get their own environmental treatment — one on a grassy field with wind blowing through it, another in a room, each with its own flavor and mood. The backgrounds can have subtle motion (grass moving, light shifting). Post-processing realism.

> "Clean catalog but like the products look elevated."
> "Marketed in a way that like you wanna fucking buy it."
> "Each one almost has its own flavor and its own experience."

**Rule:** The grid is clean. The products inside the grid are cinematic. Structure holds, content dazzles. Each album is a world, not a thumbnail.

---

## 9. Typography: MIXED — CLASSICAL TITLES, MODERN BODY

Titles and the artist name: serif, classical, carved. The feeling of text on the title page of an orchestral score. Weight and tradition.

Body text: sans-serif, modern, clean. Readable. Doesn't compete with imagery.

The overall feeling: "distant, but in a huge room." A whisper that carries because the space is so vast and quiet.

> "A mixed version of the whisper and a serif classical score."
> "Names and titles should be the more serif one."
> "Normal text should definitely be sans-serif for the more modern clean style."

**Rule:** Two type voices. The serif speaks for the artist and the work. The sans-serif speaks for the site and the information. They coexist like the piano and the flowers.

---

## 10. The One Image: CREPUSCULAR RAYS + PERSONAL MYTHOLOGY

The cloud mass with rays behind it (image 13). But not alone — layered with Mike's personal vision: carrying a skull, a white horse behind him. The hidden sun. The light you can't see but whose effects radiate outward into everything.

> "That one. Cloud mass with the rays behind it, and you carrying a scroll with a white horse behind you."

**Rule:** The entire site's visual identity can be reduced to this: a source of light hidden behind something massive, whose rays fan out and touch everything around it. The artist is the sun. The music is the light. The world is what the light transforms.
