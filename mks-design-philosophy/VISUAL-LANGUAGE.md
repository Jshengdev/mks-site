# MKS Visual Language

## Color Philosophy

### Primary Relationship: Light Against Dark

Not "light blue on black." That's the literal instruction. The principle beneath it:

**Clarity emerging from depth.**

- The dark is not emptiness. It is the vastness before dawn, the deep water beneath a surface, the closed eyes before the music starts.
- The light is not decoration. It is the thing that demands your attention. Text, titles, focal elements - they exist the way a horizon line exists against a dark sea.
- The contrast is not aesthetic preference. It is hierarchy. When everything is dark and one thing glows, you know exactly where to look.

### Color Behaviors

| Role | Principle | Not This | This Instead |
|------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Background | Depth, not absence | Flat #000000 | Blacks that breathe - subtle gradients that suggest atmosphere, like the sky 20 minutes after sunset |
| Text / Focus | Emergence | Generic white | Cool luminance - the color of light when it's the only light left. Think bioluminescence, not neon. |
| Accent | Warmth breaking through | Orange/gold as brand color | Warm tones used sparingly the way the last sliver of sun appears at the horizon - rare, earned, significant |
| Nature tones | Emotional undertone | Green = nature | The specific blue-grey of an overcast ocean. The exact warmth of light through clouds. Colors sourced from real moments, not color theory. |

## Typography Philosophy

### The Gallery Standard

> "I want the font to be a little bit more elevated. Unique. Not default SquareSpace look. Like an expensive art gallery."

**What "expensive art gallery" actually means:**

- **Restraint** - galleries use one typeface, maybe two. The confidence is in the negative space around the text, not the text itself.
- **Weight as emotion** - thin type whispers. It says: lean in. This is for you specifically.
- **Spacing as breath** - generous letter-spacing on titles. The words don't rush. They arrive.
- **No type as decoration** - every word on screen has earned its place. If it doesn't need to be there, it isn't.

### Type Behaviors

| Element | Behavior |
|---------|----------|
| Artist name | Appears slowly. Cinematic reveal. Not a logo slap - an entrance. |
| Section titles | Thin, wide-set, quiet authority. You notice them because everything else has stepped back. |
| Body text | Readable, warm, human. Not clinical. The voice of someone telling you something that matters to them. |
| Store labels | Minimal. The product imagery does the talking. Text is a whisper next to the visual. |

## Motion Philosophy

### Cinematic Pacing

Not animation for engagement. Animation as emotional choreography.

**The Entrance:**
- The name appears slowly. Not a fade-in with a CSS default. A deliberate, held reveal - the way a title card sits on screen in a film before anything else happens. You wait. The waiting is the point.

**The Scroll:**
- Movement tied to user action but not 1:1. Slight lag. Parallax that feels like depth, not like a tech demo. The world responds to you but at its own pace, like water.

**The Reveals:**
- Content doesn't pop in. It surfaces. The metaphor is: things rising from below water, clouds parting, fog clearing. The information was always there. You're just now able to see it.

**The Cursor Interaction:**
- The cursor as a point of contact between the user and the world. Moving it reveals what's beneath - scores, images, text. Not a hover effect. A discovery mechanism.
  - Concept: ocean surface as default state. Cursor movement parts it to reveal the music (literal score sheets) beneath.

## Imagery Philosophy

### Nature as Emotional Architecture

Nature elements are not backgrounds or themes. They are the structural metaphor for every emotional state the brand occupies:

| Natural Element | What It Represents | How It Functions |
|----------------|-------------------|-----------------|
| **The Ocean** | The depth of buried emotion. What lies beneath the surface you present to the world. | Used as layering device. Surface vs. depth. The cursor interaction. Things visible only when you look closer. |
| **The Sky** | Vastness. The feeling of being small inside something enormous and being okay with it. | Used for scale. Full-bleed moments where the user is enveloped. The feeling of looking up. |
| **Clouds** | Transition. The in-between state. Not clear, not storming - the held breath. | Used for movement and texture. Slow-drifting elements. The sense that the environment is alive but unhurried. |
| **The Sunset / Horizon** | The threshold. Where one thing ends and another begins. Where you stand when you're between who you were and who you'll be. | Used for color transitions between sections. The warm-to-cool shift. The moment of maximum beauty before change. |
| **Vastness itself** | The overwhelming quality of real emotion. Not curated, not contained - bigger than you. | Used for spatial design. Generous whitespace (darkspace). Elements that don't crowd. Room to feel. |

### The Score Sheets

Physical artifacts. Real paper, real handwriting, real music notation. These are the bridge between the nature metaphor and the human reality. The ocean is the feeling. The score is the proof that a human being sat down and made that feeling transmissible.

- Score sheet imagery should feel found, not presented. Like coming across sheet music left on a piano bench.
- They anchor the site in craft. This is not ambient content. Someone made this, by hand, with intention.

### Photography Rules

- **Real over generated.** The artist's authenticity is a core brand value. The audience respects that the sunsets are real, the locations are real, the person is real.
- **Unposed over composed.** Candid energy even in planned shots. The feeling of catching a moment, not constructing one.
- **Light as character.** Every photo should have a relationship with light that tells you the time of day and the emotional temperature.

## Spatial Design

### The Gallery Model

An art gallery is mostly wall. The art commands because the space around it commands first.

- **Generous margins.** Content does not touch edges.
- **Intentional emptiness.** Dark space is not wasted space. It is the silence between notes.
- **Single focal points.** Each viewport moment has one thing to look at. Not three things competing. One thing, given room.
- **Vertical rhythm.** Sections breathe. The scroll between them is not dead time - it's the inhale before the next phrase.
